by Mark Ayers, 2002

Good night, dear conscious mind—  
It's time to rest, my friend.  
At dawn, we'll meet again,  
And seek what lies ahead.

---

This poem succeeds in creating intimacy through direct address but suffers from generic execution that undermines its potential impact.

**Strengths:**

- The personification of consciousness as "dear friend" establishes warmth and creates an interesting duality between speaker and mind
- Clean structure with consistent meter creates a lullaby-like rhythm appropriate to the sleep theme
- The cyclical concept (night → dawn → seeking) effectively mirrors natural rhythms

**Critical weaknesses:**

- The language is entirely predictable—every word choice feels inevitable rather than surprising
- "What lies ahead" is a tired phrase that adds no specificity or fresh insight
- The rhyme scheme (mind/friend, again/ahead) relies on slant rhymes that feel forced rather than organic
- No concrete imagery—it operates purely in abstraction, missing opportunities for sensory engagement

**Technical issues:**

- Line 3's "At dawn, we'll meet again" creates awkward emphasis on "At" due to meter
- The progression lacks development—it states the premise but doesn't deepen or complicate it

**Recommendation:**  
Replace generic phrasing with specific, unexpected details. What exactly does this conscious mind do? What particular discoveries await? The concept has merit, but the execution needs precision and surprise to distinguish it from countless similar bedtime verses.

The poem reads like a first draft that captured an idea but hasn't yet found its unique voice.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
